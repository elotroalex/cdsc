Welcome

The Caribbean Digital Scholarship Collective comes together with a generous grant from the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation in order to develop a series of programs aimed to nurture digital scholarship in and for the Caribbean. Our wide-ranging projects reflect our acute awareness of the need for new forms of humanistic practice that are both technologically savvy and community-oriented. This need is all the more urgent as the Caribbean, and the planet itself, face the unprecedented challenges of anthropogenic climate change. Training future scholars and citizens to solve problems with cross-disciplinary, intersectional, and pragmatic approaches will impact the way our communities deal with the ever-increasing pace of change we now face, including global warming, forced migrations, racial and ethnic clashes, and the (consequent) loss of valuable historical records. Instilling holistic and agile thinking is a motivating rationale for the work we do as digital humanities scholars and a mandate for our collective. With the understanding that the cultural and historical record is now a hybrid construction—parts analog, parts digital—Caribbean digital scholarship promises to leverage computational approaches in order to draw new insights and inspire new research questions about the archive of the Atlantic world.
